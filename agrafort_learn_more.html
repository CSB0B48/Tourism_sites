Agra Fort – A Majestic Mughal Fortress

Agra Fort, also known as the Red Fort of Agra, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most remarkable Mughal forts in India. Located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, it stands as a symbol of power, grandeur, and architectural brilliance. Built primarily with red sandstone, the fort served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors before the capital shifted to Delhi.

History of Agra Fort

  • Originally built in 1080 AD by the Rajputs, the fort was later renovated by Emperor Akbar in 1565 AD.
  • It was further developed by Shah Jahan, who added several marble structures inside.
  • Shah Jahan, the builder of the Taj Mahal, was imprisoned in the fort by his son, Aurangzeb, and is said to have spent his final years gazing at the Taj Mahal from its marble balconies.

Key Highlights

  • ocation: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Built by: Emperor Akbar in 1565 AD
  • Significance: Former Mughal capital and military stronghold
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March (pleasant weather)
  • Visiting Hours: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • Entry Fee: ₹50 (Indians), ₹650 (foreigners)
