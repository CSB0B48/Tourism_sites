Taj Mahal – A Symbol of Eternal Love

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, is one of the most iconic monuments in the world. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, the Taj Mahal stands as a timeless symbol of love, devotion, and architectural brilliance. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is one of the most visited tourist attractions globally.

History of the Taj Mahal

The construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 after the death of Mumtaz Mahal, Shah Jahan’s favorite wife. It took over 20 years and the efforts of 20,000 artisans to complete this magnificent structure. Built using white marble from Makrana, Rajasthan, the Taj Mahal is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, blending elements of Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles.
Shah Jahan spent his final years imprisoned by his son Aurangzeb in the Agra Fort, where he is said to have gazed at the Taj Mahal from a distance until his death. Today, the Taj Mahal houses the tombs of both Mumtaz Mahal and Shah Jahan in its central chamber.

Key Highlights

  • Located on the banks of the Yamuna River.
  • Built in the 17th century (1632–1653).
  • Made of white marble, with intricate inlay work of semi-precious stones.
  • One of the Seven Wonders of the World.
  • Attracts millions of tourists annually.
  • Beautiful gardens, reflecting pools, and minarets enhance its grandeur.
