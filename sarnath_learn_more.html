Sarnath – The Sacred Buddhist Site

Sarnath, located near Varanasi in Uttar Pradesh, India, is one of the most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world. It is the place where Gautama Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment, known as the Dharmachakra Pravartan (Turning of the Wheel of Dharma). The site is a center of spirituality, history, and archaeological significance, attracting devotees, historians, and tourists from around the world.

Historical Significance of Sarnath

  • Buddha’s First Sermon: After attaining enlightenment in Bodh Gaya, Lord Buddha came to Sarnath and delivered his first sermon to five disciples, laying the foundation of Buddhism.
  • Spread of Buddhism: Sarnath became a major center for Buddhist teachings and monastic life under the patronage of Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE.
  • Ashoka’s Contributions: Emperor Ashoka erected several stupas, pillars, and monasteries, including the famous Ashoka Pillar, which bears the four-lion emblem, now India's national symbol.

Key Highlights

  • Location: 10 km from Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
  • Significance: Buddha’s first sermon site & Buddhist pilgrimage center
  • Main Festivals: Buddha Purnima (celebration of Buddha’s birth, enlightenment, and nirvana)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March (pleasant weather)
  • Visiting Hours: Sunrise to Sunset
